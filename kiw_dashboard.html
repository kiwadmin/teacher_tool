Phonics Bridge — 2026 Transition Note
What this level is and why it exists
Context

Beginning March 2026, KiW adopted a new phonics sequencing for Levels 1–3. New students begin with JP1 & JP2 (Level 1), then move to JP3 (Level 2).

The gap: Returning students from the old system completed only JP1 last year. They need JP2 before they can join the standard Level 2 (JP3) class alongside new students.

The Phonics Bridge is not remediation. It fills exactly one gap. Once these students complete JP2, they move into the regular Level 2 class.

This level will phase out naturally once no more old-system students remain. Both cohorts converge at Level 2 and proceed together from that point.

Extended Review — G&S 1
Every 6th R&C class (roughly once per month) · G&S 1 — T/Th R&C classes

Consolidate what students have read across 5–6 units. Builds fluency, retrieval practice, and cross-text discussion skills.

How to Run an ER Class
① Warm-up (5 min) — Quick recall: "What did we read last week? What was it about?"
② Language focus (10 min) — Teacher highlights 3–5 vocabulary or grammar items from the covered texts.
③ Discussion task (30–35 min) — Main activity per the session guide below. Always ends with students speaking.
④ Wrap-up (5 min) — One sentence per student: "The most interesting thing I read this period was…"
March–April · RB1 Units 1–3
Retell stories from texts. Teacher prompts: "What happened? Who was it about? What surprised you?"
May · RB1 Units 4–7
Vocabulary review — match words to definitions from 4 units. Then pair discussion: favourite text and why.
June · RB1 Units 8–12
Compare two animals/topics from different texts. Use: "Both… / But… / I think…"
Sep–Oct · RB2 Units 1–4
Opinion sharing: "Which character would you want to meet? Which place would you visit?" Use evidence.
Nov–Dec · RB2 Units 5–9
Extended discussion: pick one text, explain it to a partner who didn't read it carefully.
Jan–Feb · RB2 Units 10–12
Full review: favourite RB2 text. Write 2 sentences + share with class.

Extended Review — G&S 4
Every 12th R&C class (roughly every 4 weeks) · G&S 4 — MWF R&C classes

High-level academic consolidation. Argument analysis, personal voice development, and synthesis across multiple texts.

How to Run an ER Class
① Warm-up (5 min) — Quick recall: "What did we read last week? What was it about?"
② Language focus (10 min) — Teacher highlights 3–5 vocabulary or grammar items from the covered texts.
③ Discussion task (30–35 min) — Main activity per the session guide below. Always ends with students speaking.
④ Wrap-up (5 min) — One sentence per student: "The most interesting thing I read this period was…"
March–April · RB7 Units 1–3
Evaluative discussion: "How strong is this argument? What evidence would make it stronger?"
May · RB7 Units 4–6
Synthesis: find a connecting idea across 3 texts. Write a claim that links all three.
June–July · RB7 Units 7–9
Counter-argument practice: take a position from a text and argue the opposite.
Jul–Aug · RB7 Units 10–12
Academic vocabulary: build a class glossary from RB7. Students define and exemplify.
Aug–Sep · RB8 Units 1–3
Personal voice: connect a text's argument to your own experience or opinion.
Sep–Oct · RB8 Units 4–6
Debate: divide class by text position. Each side summarises their text's argument.
Nov · RB8 Units 7–9
Portfolio selection: choose one text that most influenced your thinking. Write 1 paragraph why.
Dec–Feb · RB8 Units 10–12
Final consolidation: full year synthesis — present your most developed argument to the group.
